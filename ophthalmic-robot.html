Dynamics of Robotic Arm Motion Mechanics for Ophthalmic Surgery

Jan 2024 - Apr 2024

CAD/CAM Control Engineering Python MATLAB Mechanical Design

Overview

Investigated the dynamics of robotic arm motion to enhance surgical precision and efficiency, specifically in ophthalmic operations. Developed a comprehensive mathematical model and utilized CAD software for the simulation of robotic arm behaviors.

This study focused on the mechanical design, involving a 6-bar linkage with a sliding axis, and applied principles from Gruebler's equation to determine the 6 degrees of freedom necessary for precise surgical manipulation.

Technical Approach

  • Designed precise ophthalmic-surgery robotic arm with 6-bar linkage mechanism in SolidWorks
  • Applied GD&T (Geometric Dimensioning and Tolerancing) per ANSI Y14.5 standards
  • Derived kinematics and kinetics using Gruebler's equation for 6-DOF system
  • Ran dynamics simulations to map workspace and identify structural singularities
  • Characterized workspace with identified safe regions through closed-form modeling
  • Performed CAD-based dynamic simulation for validation

Results

Achieved precise, stable motion over a wider operative range as measured by a characterized workspace with identified structural singularities and safe regions. The findings contribute significantly to advancements in robot-assisted surgeries, offering insights into improving robotic design and control algorithms.
